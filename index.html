About Me

I am currently pursuing an MSc in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Edinburgh, focusing on Natural Language Processing. I received my MEng in Electrical Engineering from the University of Bristol with the thesis topic of Video Compression Using Deep Neural Networks. This is my professional website, containing information about some of my past projects as well as relevant links.

Past Projects

Video Compression Using Deep Neural Networks

As part of this research thesis, deep learning-based enhancements were applied on a new video coding standard. State-of-the-art deep video compression architectures, trained on compressed video content, were used to achieve enhanced video quality for the same bitrates.

Robotic Surgery Tool Tracking Using Deep Learning

This research internship involved the collection and annotation of images from a wide range of robotic surgery recordings. The data was used to train an object detection model tracking robotic manipulators in areas of limited visibility.

Autonomous Solver for Ball-in-a-Maze Puzzles Using Computer Vision

The aim of this project was to build a computer application that could solve a ball-in-a-maze puzzle autonomously, using a combination of software and hardware components. The whole system was based on a feedback loop, with a control algorithm determining the optimal motor movements based on input from a camera.
