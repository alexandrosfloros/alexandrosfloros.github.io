About me

I am an Electrical and Electronic Engineering MEng student, currently studying at the University of Bristol. I am mainly interested in innovative projects involving machine learning and software development, back-end as well as front-end. Each one of my projects started out as a new learning experience with a clear motivation and goal in mind, and I like to regularly keep them up-to-date by implementing additional features and improvements. I always look forward for ways of expanding my knowledge and exploring unique ideas to deliver a final product, and, as such, I am open to any work and career opportunities. Further information about me, my background, and my recent work can be found on this website.

Recent projects

Logic Circuit Solver

A UI application used to analyse logic circuits and solve a variety of problems often encountered in digital electronics, using Boolean expressions, truth tables and Karnaugh maps. It allows for great flexibility and experimentation with different inputs and variables, and is particularly useful for educational purposes.

Geometric Shape Analyser

A UI application used to analyse a set of points on the plane and identify different geometric shapes, based on their mathematical properties. It makes use of a variety of mathematical concepts and theorems from linear algebra and Euclidean geometry. Numerous optimisation techniques are utilised to reduce code complexity and improve performance.

Offline Encrypted Database

A UI application used to safely store sensitive data locally, behind a login system. It allows the user to organise their data by registering and managing multiple accounts. Password verification is achieved with sha256, and an encryption algorithm is used to read and store user data in a spreadsheet database.

Rolling Ball Control

A simulation of an autonomous ball-in-a-maze puzzle solution algorithm, designed as part of an overall system, using a combination of software and hardware. The system aims to sucessfully solve the puzzle by tilting the board and hence moving the ball through the maze, whilst avoiding any obstacles.
